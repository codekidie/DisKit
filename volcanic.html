VOLCANIC ERUPTION

Explosive volcanoes blast hot solid and molten rock fragments and gases into the air. As a result, ashflows can occur on all sides of a volcano and ash can fall hundreds of miles downwind. Dangerous mudflows and floods can occur in valleys leading away from volcanoes. If you live near a known volcano, active or dormant, be prepared to follow volcano safety instructions from your local emergency officials.

PREPARATION TIPS

What should I do if I live in an area at risk from VOLCANIC ERUPTION?

  • Learn about your community warning systems and emergency plans.
  • Teach family members how to shut off water, gas and electricity to the house.
  • Purchase at least one multi-purpose dry chemical fire extinguisher.
  • Assemble and maintain an emergency preparedness kit.
  • Install smoke detectors and change the batteries every six months.
  • Watch the patterns of storm water drainage on slopes near your home, especially where runoff water converges.
